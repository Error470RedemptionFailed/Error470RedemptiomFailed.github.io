Cybersecurity Threats

Cyber threats are various forms of harmful activities designed to damage or disrupt digital life. Here are some common types of cyber threats:

  • Malware: This is malicious software, such as viruses, worms, trojans, and ransomware, that is designed to harm a computer or network.

  • Phishing: A deceptive attempt, usually through email, to steal sensitive personal information like usernames, passwords, and credit card details by pretending to be a trustworthy entity.

  • Denial-of-Service (DoS) and Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS) Attacks: These attacks aim to overwhelm the resources of a system, making it unable to respond to service requests, which can lead to a shutdown of the system.

  • Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) Attacks: This type of attack allows a hacker to eavesdrop on the communication between two parties and intercept or alter the data being exchanged.

  • SQL Injection: This attack involves inserting malicious SQL statements into an entry field for execution, to manipulate a database and access unauthorized information.

  • Cross-Site Scripting (XSS): An attacker injects malicious scripts into content from otherwise trusted websites to execute on the end user’s browser.

  • Social Engineering: Manipulating individuals into breaking normal security procedures to gain unauthorized access to systems, networks, or physical locations, or for financial gain.

  • Password Attacks: Attempting to retrieve or guess a user’s password to gain access to their accounts and personal information.

  • Insider Threats: Risks posed by individuals from within the organization, such as employees or contractors, who may have malicious intent or inadvertently cause harm.

  • AI-Powered Attacks: Sophisticated attacks that use artificial intelligence to automate and scale cybercriminal activities.
